Tempura

Tempura (天ぷら or 天麩羅, tenpura, [tempɯɾa]) is a typical Japanese dish usually consisting of seafood, meat and vegetables that have been battered and deep fried. The dish was introduced by Iberians in Nagasaki through the fritter-cooking techniques in the 16th century. The name "tempura" may originate from the Spanish Témporas or from the Portuguese Têmporas, which refer to the Ember Days, during which no meat is consumed, or from the Portuguese word tempêro, meaning “seasoning”.
